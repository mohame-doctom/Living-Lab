Research Pillars

Latest Research

Mitochondrial Efficiency in Epidermal Cells

Recent findings on mitochondrial biogenesis pathways in epidermal keratinocytes reveal that targeted NAD+ precursor supplementation — specifically nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN) — increases SIRT1-mediated mitophagy by 14% in controlled murine models. The impact of chronic blue light (HEV) exposure on Complex IV electron transport chain efficiency is now measurable through non-invasive transepidermal biomarkers, opening new diagnostic windows for assessing cumulative photoaging damage before clinical signs manifest.

Clinical Insights

Synthetic Peptide Chains & Collagen Synthesis

Phase III clinical data from the Zurich Dermatological Institute confirms that tri-peptide-38 sequences, delivered via liposomal encapsulation, stimulate type I and III procollagen synthesis at rates 2.3× greater than retinoid-only protocols — critically, without triggering the NF-κB inflammatory cascade. This marks the first replicable, non-retinoid pathway to measurable neocollagenesis in a clinical setting, with implications for individuals who are retinoid-intolerant or seeking to minimize barrier disruption.

Speculative Science

Bio-Printing Living Skin Tissues

4D bio-printing of scaffold-integrated dermal equivalents has moved from theoretical to pre-clinical, with researchers at MIT's Koch Institute successfully printing multi-layered epidermal constructs that self-organize over 72 hours. These "living mesh" constructs maintain vascularization potential and respond to external growth factor signals — suggesting a future where personalized skin grafts, tailored to an individual's inflammatory profile, could replace traditional wound dressings and serve as a delivery platform for chronobiological drug release.

Featured Compound

Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)

A precursor to NADH and NADPH — the essential cofactors for cellular energy production and DNA repair. Niacinamide operates upstream of NAD+ synthesis via the salvage pathway, making it one of the most direct topical interventions for age-related NAD+ decline in epidermal cells. At concentrations of 4–5%, peer-reviewed evidence demonstrates measurable improvements in barrier function, sebum regulation, and hyperpigmentation reduction within 8–12 weeks.
